Soryn

➤ Goddess of Fate

Lore

Soryn, the Goddes of Fate, is a mysterious and quiet deity who silently weaves the fates of mortals and gods alike. Unlike Aelos, whose fortune can shift like the wind, Soryn’s power lies in the unseen forces of inevitability. She is often depicted as a solemn, ethereal figure, clothed in robes that shimmer like starlight, her eyes reflecting the endless paths of time.

According to legend, Soryn was born from the first breath of creation, when time itself began to move forward. Her birthright was the control over the flow of fate—ensuring that all things come to their destined end. Her is said to dwell in the Nexus of Threads, a vast and cosmic loom where the lives of mortals are spun into intricate patterns that cannot be undone.

Despite his quiet nature, Soryn is far from passive. Her subtly influences the course of events, guiding or gently shifting the threads of fate when necessary. Her is the patron of prophets, seers, and those who seek to uncover the mysteries of the future. Her followers believe that while fate is inevitable, it can be understood and even subtly altered by those who understand its patterns.

Worship of Soryn is both revered and feared, for while she governs destiny, she also represents the unavoidable truths of life: death, endings, and the unknown. Her temples are serene places, often filled with tapestries and patterns representing the interwoven paths of all beings. The clergy of Soryn, known as the Weavers of Destiny, are known for their wisdom and their ability to interpret the subtle signs of fate.

Goal

Soryn seeks to ensure that the balance of fate is maintained, that every soul follows the path woven for them by the strands of destiny, though his hand may subtly guide or unravel those threads when needed.
