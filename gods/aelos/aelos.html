Aelos

➤ God of Fortune

Lore

Aelos, the God of Fortune, is a deity of contrasts. Known for his whimsical and unpredictable nature. Aelos delights inthe unpredicitability og life, weaving threads of fate and chance into a tapestry that defines the destinies of mortals and gods alike. He is often depicted as a handsome figure with a celestial features, clothed in shimmering garments that reflect the changing hues of the dawn and dusk.

According to legend, Aelos was born from the first roll of cosmic dice at the dawn of creation. The resulting number signigied his birth, granting him dominion over fortune and luck. As the overseer of chance, Aelos wanders the realms, bestowing blessings and curses with a flick of his wrist, guided by the onigmatic whims of fate.

Worship of Aelos is widespread among adventurers, gamblers, merchants, and those who live on the edge of uncertainty. His temples are adorned with symbols of luck—four-leaf clovers, horseshoes, and golden dice—and are places where followers seek his favor through offerings and games of chance. The Luckbringers, his clergy, are known for their charismatic and daring personalities, often serving as advisers, fortune-tellers, and mediators in conflicts.

Goal

Aelos seeks to ensure that luck and fortune smile upo those who worship him, bringing prosperity and success in their endeavors.
