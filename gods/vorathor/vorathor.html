Vorathor

➤ God of Death

Lore

Vorathor was once a celestial servant of balance, tasked with guiding souls from life to death. Over time, he grew resentful of the living for clinging so desperately to life and of the gods who interfered with the natural order by granting resurrection or immortality.

Turning from his celestial origins, Vorathor descended into a nihilistic madness. He declared that death should not only be inevitable but final. He began severing souls' ties to the afterlife, trapping them in his domain of Endless Quiet, a gray, featureless expanse devoid of sensation, thought, or memory.

The other gods fear Vorathor for his power over the cycle of life and death, but they hesitate to act against him, as his role is still vital to maintaining the world's balance.

Bargain: The Coin of Death

It is whispered among mortals that, upon thy deathbed, when Vorathor, the dread God of Death, cometh to bear thee unto the shadowed realm of the departed, thou mayest entreat him for a wager most perilous—a casting of the Death Coin. Once the challenge be spoken, it may not be undone, and the outcome shall be immutable, binding as the eternal stars.

Vorathor shall then draw forth his fateful coin, inscribed with the solemn phrases: “Memento Mori” (Remember thy death) and “Memet Vivre” (Live again). With a flick of his hand, he shall send it spinning through the air, a decision between life and eternal torment.

Should the coin fall upon “Memet Vivre,” thy soul shall be restored unto the realm of the living. This reprieve is granted with but one purpose—that thou might live thy life anew, with greater wisdom and fuller heart. Yet beware, for should it fall upon “Memento Mori,” thy fate shall be grim indeed. Thou shalt be cast irrevocably into the depths of Vorathor’s dominion, punished for thy audacious hope of resurrection. In that dread realm shalt thou languish, a shadow among shadows, until the Lord of Death himself show thee mercy. But hear this well: some say that death is the truest mercy. To defy such mercy is to forfeit thy peace; thus shalt thou endure, not in rest, but in endless, unyielding existence for eternity.
