Thornsinger

Silken voice. Crimson eyes. A haunting melody echoing down the corridor. When you hear the Thornsinger's tune, it’s already too late.

They are the maestros of misery, the composers of corruption. Gifted with both vampiric blood and bardic power, Thornsingers do not fight—they perform. Each battle is a composition, every scream a harmony. And their final note? Always silence.

Used to enthrall nobles, break prisoners, or serenade executions, they are the honeyed blade of House Sangrelis—cutting deeper than any steel.

❤️ HP 68
🛡️ AC 15
📈 CR 5
🏃‍♂️ Speed 30 ft.

⚔️ Attacks

  • Sonic Lash +6 to hit, 60 ft. range
    • Hit: 2d6 thunder damage
    • The target must make a DC 14 Constitution saving throw or be deafened for 1 minute.
  • Siren Song (Recharge 5-6):
    • Each creature within 30 feet that can hear must make a DC 15 Wisdom saving throw.
    • On a failed save, the creature is Charmed for 1 minute. They repeat the save at the end of each turn.

🧠 Special Abilities

  • Vampiric Voice: When a creature becomes Charmed by the Thornsinger, it takes 1d6 psychic damage as whispers claw at its mind.
Clad in violet silks, their faces half-hidden by veils of spun shadow, Thornsingers are rarely seen and never forgotten. Each word they speak is laced with temptation—and torment.
